About Me

I am a 19 year old student attending the University of Northern Colorado, with aspirations to become a developer of some sort. I was born in West Virginia, but Colorado is my home. Along with my love for technology, I am an avid sports fan. Up through high school, I played: soccer, basketball, and baseball. I can’t really say I have a favorite, I always liked what was in season. The teams I often get disappointed by consist of the Washington Redskins, Colorado Rockies, and Miami Heat. Geographically, my fandom doesn’t make much sense, but it’s not about the location...it’s about the the pride and heart you have for your team, right?


Computers have always been a fascinating machine to me and the more I learn, the deeper the black hole of confusion I fall into. Climbing out of that black hole however, is an adventure I hope never ends. I didn’t actually find out about programming, and what it does, until my junior year of high school. I never really thought about how these machines worked, I just accepted that the Internet, and computers were created by the byte gods with black magic, and I carried on editing the configuration files to my favorite programs. Then 11th grade came along and I figured I should try to figure out something to do with the rest of my life. My future was shaped by a simple Google search “least stressful and highest paying jobs.” As I looked through a website that Google recommended I found software development, and I was off. Thanks Google.


I enjoy developing websites, programming games, coding task automation programs, and eating french fries. Most of all, I love learning what else I am capable of doing with these computers that are running our world.
